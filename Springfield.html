Springfield, Illinois

  • Population: Approximately 113,000 (2023 estimate). Springfield is the sixth-largest city in Illinois. [1]
  • Founded: Settled in 1818, incorporated as a town in 1832 and as a city in 1840. [1]
  • Region: Central Illinois, located along the Sangamon River. Springfield is the county seat of Sangamon County. [1]
  • Classification: Urban city, state capital of Illinois, and a regional center for government, history, and healthcare. [1]
  • Average Household Income: ~$59,000 (U.S. Census Bureau, 2023). The city features a mix of government, healthcare, and service sector jobs. [2]

Springfield is best known as the home of Abraham Lincoln, the 16th President of the United States. The city is rich in history, featuring the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum, Lincoln's Home National Historic Site, and his tomb. As the state capital, Springfield is the center of Illinois government and hosts the Illinois State Capitol building.

The city offers a blend of historic charm and modern amenities, with a vibrant downtown, numerous parks, and cultural attractions. Springfield's economy is driven by government, healthcare, education, and tourism. The city is also known for its Route 66 heritage and annual state fair, drawing visitors from across the region.
