Chicago, Illinois

  • Population: Approximately 2,697,000 (2023 estimate). Chicago is the most populous city in Illinois and the third largest in the United States. [1]
  • Founded: Incorporated as a city in 1837. The area was first settled in the 1780s and grew rapidly in the 19th century. [1]
  • Region: Northeastern Illinois, on the southwestern shore of Lake Michigan. Chicago is the anchor of the Chicago metropolitan area, also known as Chicagoland. [1]
  • Classification: Major global city (Alpha global city), urban metropolis, and county seat of Cook County. [1]
  • Average Household Income: ~$75,000 (U.S. Census Bureau, 2023). Chicago has a diverse economy and a wide range of income levels across its neighborhoods. [2]

Chicago is renowned for its stunning skyline, world-class museums, and vibrant arts and music scenes. The city is a major center for finance, technology, manufacturing, and transportation, and is home to two of the nation’s busiest airports. Chicago’s neighborhoods are famous for their diversity, culture, and cuisine—including the iconic deep-dish pizza and Chicago-style hot dogs.

The city played a pivotal role in American history, from the Great Chicago Fire of 1871 to the rise of jazz and blues, and the development of modern architecture. Chicago is also known for its sports teams, including the Chicago Cubs, White Sox, Bulls, Bears, and Blackhawks. With its beautiful lakefront, extensive park system, and vibrant downtown, Chicago is a dynamic city that attracts millions of visitors each year. [1]
